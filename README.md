📊 Sales Overview Analytics Dashboard — Power BI

An end-to-end Sales Analytics Dashboard built using Power BI, analyzing customer behavior, product performance, regional trends, and profitability across segments. This project demonstrates advanced data modeling, DAX calculations, interactive slicers, KPI design, and business storytelling aligned with real-world retail and sales analytics use cases.


🚀 Project Overview
The dashboard provides a 360° analytical view of sales performance, enabling business stakeholders to:

Track overall revenue, profit, quantity, and customer metrics at a glance
Analyze product sub-category performance and identify profit vs loss areas
Understand regional and segment-wise sales distribution
Evaluate shipping mode efficiency and its impact on sales
Explore category-level trends using a dynamic waterfall chart

🧭 Dashboard Breakdown
📦 Sales & Profit by Sub-Category

Compares Sum of Sales vs Sum of Profit across all product sub-categories
Phones lead in sales at $0.33M, followed by Chairs ($0.22M) and Storage ($0.20M)
Highlights loss-making sub-categories like Tables (-$0.02M profit) — critical for strategic decision making
Helps identify which products to promote or discontinue

🗺️ Sales by Region

Horizontal bar chart showing regional performance across West, East, Central, and South
West region leads with the highest sales volume, followed closely by East
Useful for territory planning and regional sales strategy

👤 Sales by Segment

Both a bar chart and donut chart visualize performance across Consumer, Corporate, and Home Office segments
Consumer segment dominates at 50.57% ($1.16M), followed by Corporate at 30.73% ($0.71M) and Home Office at 18.71% ($0.43M)
Helps prioritize marketing and sales efforts by segment

📂 Sales by Category (Waterfall Chart)

Displays contribution of Technology, Furniture, and Office Supplies to total sales
Technology and Office Supplies show positive contribution (green), Furniture shows mixed performance
Excellent for storytelling around category-level revenue drivers

🚚 Sales by Ship Mode

Line chart analyzing sales performance across Standard Class, Second Class, First Class, and Same Day
Standard Class dominates with over $1M in sales, while Same Day shipping has the lowest volume
Useful for logistics and fulfillment strategy

📁 Dataset Information

Source: Publicly available retail sales dataset (Superstore-style) adapted for analytical practice
Time Period: 2021 onwards
Fields Include: Order ID, Order Date, Ship Date, Ship Mode, Customer ID, Segment, Region, Category, Sub-Category, Product Name, Sales, Quantity, Discount, Profit
